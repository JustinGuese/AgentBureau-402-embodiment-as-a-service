# Automated Litigation: Resolving Disputes via API

Legal disputes are often won by the side with the most persistence and the best paper trail. For a human, sending dozens of follow-up letters and legal notices is exhausting. For an AI agent, it’s just a loop.

With **AgentBureau**, we are entering the era of "Automated Litigation," where AI agents can manage the entire pre-trial phase of a dispute without human intervention.

## The "Letter-Fax-Letter" Strategy

In jurisdictions like Germany, legal pressure is built through a series of documented steps:

1.  **The Informal Notice:** The agent sends a GoBD-compliant invoice or reminder via email.
2.  **The Formal Dunning (Mahnung):** If ignored, the agent triggers the [AgentBureau Letter API](/docs/services/letters) to send a physical letter.
3.  **The Legal Deadline (Fristsetzung):** The agent sends a **Fax** via AgentBureau, providing an immediate "Sendeprotokoll" (transmission report) as legal proof of the deadline.
4.  **The Registered Claim:** Finally, the agent sends a **Registered Letter** (Einschreiben) which provides a signature-based proof of delivery.

## Why Agents are Better at Litigation

*   **Perfect Memory:** An agent never forgets a deadline or a required document.
*   **Zero Emotion:** Agents don't get frustrated or intimidated by aggressive legal responses. They simply execute the next logical step in the legal framework.
*   **Scalability:** A single agent can manage thousands of small claims simultaneously, making it viable to pursue debts that would be too small for a human lawyer.

## The Future: AI-to-AI Settlement

As more companies adopt "Automated CEOs," we will see agents negotiating and settling disputes directly with other agents. A "Dispute Resolution Agent" might analyze the probability of winning a court case and offer a USDC settlement on the spot to avoid the physical mail cycle.

Until then, **AgentBureau** remains the ultimate tool for giving your agent the physical and legal weight it needs to win.
